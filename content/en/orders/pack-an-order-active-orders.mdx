<Section icon={Map} title="Step-by-Step Warehouse Workflow">

Success in the warehouse depends on a repeatable, logical workflow. Beginners often struggle with "back-and-forth" syndrome, where they run to a shelf for one item, return to pack it, and then realize they need another item from the same shelf. Following a structured process reduces idle time and increases your hourly Coin yield.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Analyze the Active Ticket">
    Open your current order and memorize the items. Look for specific keywords like "Clicker" or "Salted Butter." If you have multiple orders, cycle through them to find overlapping shelf locations.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Plan the Shelf Route">
    Move toward the shelf sections that contain the majority of your requested items. Use your knowledge of the warehouse layout to take the shortest path, avoiding obstacles and other players.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Verify and Collect">
    Approach the shelf and use the interaction key (E or Left Click) to pick up the item. If two items look similar, use the Inspect function (Right Click or Y) to confirm the product name matches the ticket.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Pack and Finalize">
    Return to the packing area. Use the scroll wheel to select the correct item from your inventory and interact with the box to pack it. Once the checklist is green, move the box to the delivery truck.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Mispacking">
Packing an incorrect item will not only waste the item but may require you to restart the box or remove the item, significantly hurting your completion time. Always inspect uncertain toys.
</Callout>

| Workflow Stage | Primary Action | Common Mistake |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Retrieval** | Collecting toys from shelves | Picking up items without checking the order |
| **Verification** | Inspecting item names | Relying solely on the toy's color or shape |
| **Inventory** | Managing carried items | Forgetting to scroll to the correct item |
| **Loading** | Moving boxes to the truck | Leaving completed boxes at the packing station |

<Section icon={Swords} title="Co-op vs Solo Strategies">

The game supports both solo play and collaborative warehouse crews. Depending on your team size, your approach to **pack an order active orders** should shift. In a team, the biggest threat to efficiency is "doubling up"—where two players fetch the same item because they aren't communicating which ticket they are working on.

**Solo Strategy:**
In solo mode, you are the picker, packer, and loader. Your focus should be on inventory management. Since you don't have anyone to help, use the R key constantly to ensure you are never walking with an empty inventory. If you are heading to the back of the warehouse for Order A, check if Order B needs anything from that area.

**Multiplayer Strategy:**
Teams should assign specific roles to prevent chaos. A dedicated "Packer" stays near the boxes and trucks, while "Pickers" sprint to the shelves.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Controls and Navigation Reference">

Mastering the controls is essential for maintaining a high "Orders Per Hour" (OPH) rate. Most interactions are mapped to the E key or Left Click, but advanced players make heavy use of the secondary keys to manage their inventory without stopping their movement.

| Input | Action | Pro Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **E / Left Click** | Pick up / Pack item | Double-tap to quickly grab and stow |
| **Q** | Drop current item | Drop wrong items near their shelves, not in aisles |
| **R** | Cycle active orders | Check this every time you finish an item |
| **Scroll Wheel** | Switch held items | Switch to the next item while running back |
| **Right Click / Y** | Inspect product | Use this for the 'Matcha' vs 'Wasabi' items |

Proper navigation also involves understanding the shelf "zones." The warehouse is typically organized by item type (e.g., Toys, Electronics, Food). Memorizing which aisle contains the "Matcha Dumplings" will save you more time than any speed upgrade ever could.

<Callout type="tip" title="Navigation Tip">
