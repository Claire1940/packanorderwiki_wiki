Success in the warehouse begins with understanding your environment and mastering the interaction system. <Section icon={Target} title="Order Management: Fulfilling Pack an Order Toys">

Managing your workflow is the difference between a bronze and a gold ranking on the leaderboards. Every customer ticket acts as a checklist. When you interact with **pack an order toys**, you aren't just grabbing random objects; you are matching specific names and quantities. For instance, an order for Becca might require one Red Squishy and three Salted Butter items. If you only bring two Salted Butters, the box will not close, and you cannot proceed to the delivery stage.

The "Inspect" feature is your best friend during complex orders. Many toys share similar silhouettes or color palettes. By using Right Click or the Y button, you can confirm the item's identity before it ever touches your inventory. This prevents the "Incorrect Item" error that often plagues beginners.

<Section icon={Shield} title="Multiplayer Strategy and Co-op Roles">

While solo play is a great way to learn the mechanics, **pack an order toys** truly shines in multiplayer. When working with a team, the "all-in-one" approach is inefficient. Instead, you should divide the warehouse into specific roles. This specialization allows each player to focus their upgrades on what matters most for their specific task.

| Role | Primary Duty | Best Upgrade |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Picker** | Fetches toys from shelves | Speed & Reach |
| **The Packer** | Matches items to boxes | Carry Capacity |
| **The Loader** | Moves boxes to the truck | Speed |

In a three-person team, the Picker stays in the aisles and drops items near the packing station. The Packer stays at the station, verifies the items against the ticket, and fills the boxes. The Loader handles the final leg of the journey, ensuring the truck is never waiting for cargo. This conveyor-belt style of play is the secret behind the record-breaking times seen on the global leaderboards.

<Callout type="tip" title="Team Communication">
Use the in-game chat or voice comms to announce which order you are working on. There is no bigger time-waste than two players fetching the same **pack an order toys** for the same ticket.
</Callout>
