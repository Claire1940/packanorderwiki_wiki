<Section icon={Package} title="Maximizing Carry Capacity and Reach">

While Speed is straightforward, the interaction between Carry Capacity and Reach is where advanced players optimize their runs. **Pack an order coin upgrades** in these categories allow you to change *how* you play the game, rather than just doing the same things faster.

**Carry Capacity Mechanics:**
Increasing your capacity allows you to hold more toys, such as Red Squishies, Matcha Dumplings, or Clickers. This is vital when an order requires 3 or 4 of the same item. Instead of making four trips, a high Carry Capacity allows for a single shelf-to-box run. Use the **Scroll Wheel** to cycle through your held items to ensure you pack them in the correct sequence.

**Reach Mechanics:**
Reach is often misunderstood. It doesn't just let you grab things from further away; it widens the "Interaction Hitbox." This means you don't have to be perfectly centered in front of a shelf to trigger the "E" or "Left Click" prompt. In high-pressure situations, this millisecond saved on positioning adds up across a 10-item order.

| Upgrade Level | Carry Slots | Reach Distance | Approx. Cost (Coins) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Level 1** | 1 Slot | Base | 0 (Initial) |
| **Level 2** | 2 Slots | +10% | 500 |
| **Level 3** | 3 Slots | +25% | 1,250 |
| **Level 4** | 4 Slots | +40% | 3,000 |
| **Level 5** | 5 Slots | +60% | 7,500 |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Order Selection">

  <Step num="2" title="The Collection Run">

  <Step num="5" title="Reinvestment">

  </Step>

| Feature Type | Persistence | Primary Function | Example |

**When to choose Abilities over Upgrades:**
If your base stats (Speed/Reach/Capacity) are already at Level 3 or 4, the cost of the next upgrade becomes significantly higher. This is usually the best time to look at the Ability tree. An ability that helps you locate items (Search Radar) might save more time than a 5% increase in Speed if you often get lost in the warehouse.
