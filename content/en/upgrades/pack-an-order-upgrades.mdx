<Section icon={Target} title="Priority Rankings for Solo and Co-op Play">

The value of each attribute shifts depending on whether you are working alone or coordinating with a team. Solo players must handle the entire pipeline—from reading tickets to loading the delivery truck—making inventory space critical. Co-op teams, however, can specialize in specific zones, making interaction range and movement speed far more valuable.

### Solo Player Upgrade Path
For solo operations, minimizing the total number of trips between distant shelves and the packing station is the key to high-efficiency runs.

| Priority | Stat Target | Strategic Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Speed** | Reduces baseline travel time across long warehouse routes |
| **2** | **Carry Capacity** | Allows carrying multiple order components in a single trip |
| **3** | **Reach** | Minimizes micro-positioning adjustments when grabbing items |

### Co-op Team Upgrade Path
In organized teams, players should divide duties. Runners need high mobility, while dedicated packers benefit from grabbing items from a distance.

| Priority | Stat Target | Strategic Reasoning |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **Speed** | Keeps runners moving between designated shelf zones quickly |
| **2** | **Reach** | Allows packers to grab delivered items without leaving the table |
| **3** | **Carry Capacity** | Lower priority as items are constantly handed off |

<Callout type="warning" title="Team Coordination">
In multiplayer, do not have everyone upgrade identically. Designate one player as a high-capacity runner and another as a high-reach packer for optimal synergy.
</Callout>
