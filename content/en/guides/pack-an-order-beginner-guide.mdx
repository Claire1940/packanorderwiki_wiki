<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step Operations Guide">

To maximize your earnings, you must follow a disciplined workflow. The warehouse can become chaotic when multiple orders arrive simultaneously, but following these structured steps will keep your productivity high.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initialize Your Session">
    Enter the warehouse and decide between Solo or Multiplayer mode. Solo play is excellent for learning item locations, while Multiplayer allows for specialized roles and faster completion times.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Analyze the Customer Ticket">
    Check the active order ticket on your screen. It lists the customer name, order number, and specific toys required. For example, an order for "Becca" might require 1 Red Squishy and 2 Matcha Dumplings.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute the Search Phase">
    Move to the warehouse shelves. Use your knowledge of the layout to find the requested products. If you are unsure about an item, use **Right Click** or the **Y button** to inspect it before picking it up.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Collection and Management">
    Press **E** or **Left Click** to pick up items. If you have upgraded your **Carry Capacity**, you can hold multiple toys at once. Use the **Scroll Wheel** to cycle through your inventory so the correct item is "active" when you reach the box.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="The Packing Process">
    Approach the order box and press **E** to pack the active item. Ensure the quantity matches the ticket. If you accidentally pick up the wrong item, press **Q** to drop it immediately and clear your inventory.
  </Step>
  <Step num="6" title="Final Delivery">
    Once the box is full and the checklist is green, interact with the completed order to load it onto the delivery truck. This final step triggers the Coin reward and clears the order from your queue.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Order Accuracy">
Packing the wrong item doesn't just waste time—it prevents the order from being completed. Always verify the item name via the **Inspect** tool if the toy shapes look similar on the shelf.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Zap} title="Essential Controls and Interactions">

The control scheme in Pack an Order is designed for speed. Mastering these inputs is the first hurdle for any new player. The game supports both Keyboard/Mouse and Gamepad inputs, allowing for flexible playstyles across different devices.

| Input (PC) | Action | Practical Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **E / Left Click** | Pick Up / Pack | Use this for all primary interactions with toys and boxes. |
| **Q** | Drop Item | Drop unwanted items near their original shelves to keep the floor clear. |
| **R** | Cycle Orders | Switch between active tickets to plan a route that collects items for two orders. |
| **Scroll Wheel** | Cycle Inventory | Switch to the next required item while walking toward the packing station. |
| **Right Click / Y** | Inspect Item | Use this on high shelves where item labels might be obscured. |

<Callout type="success" title="Pro Control Tip">
You can cycle through orders using **R** while you are already at a shelf. This allows you to see if any other active customers need items from your current location, effectively doubling your efficiency per trip.
</Callout>

</Section>

Coins earned from deliveries should be reinvested into your worker's stats. There are three core upgrades that dictate how fast you can clear the warehouse. Choosing the right order for these upgrades is vital for early-game progression.
