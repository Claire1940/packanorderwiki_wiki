<Section icon={Map} title="Warehouse Fundamentals: The Pack an Order Walkthrough">

To succeed in this fast-paced Roblox warehouse simulator, you must understand the core mechanics that drive your progression. The primary goal is to fulfill customer orders by finding specific toys on shelves, packing them into boxes, and delivering them to the loading dock. Every successful delivery earns you **Coins**, which are essential for unlocking permanent worker upgrades and special abilities.

The warehouse environment is designed with a bright, warm orange aesthetic, but don't let the cheerful colors distract you. Efficiency is the name of the game. Whether you are playing solo or with friends, your completion time is tracked on the global leaderboards. A proper **pack an order walkthrough** begins with learning the layout of the shelves so you can navigate to items like Matcha Dumplings or Red Squishies without hesitation.

| Step | Action | Objective |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Read Ticket | Identify required items and quantities |
| 2 | Locate Shelf | Move to the correct warehouse section |
| 3 | Pick & Inspect | Verify the product name before collection |
| 4 | Pack Order | Place matching items into the shipping box |
| 5 | Load Truck | Deliver the finished box to the vehicle |

<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering Controls and Order Management">

Precision is just as important as speed. Using the wrong input or picking up the incorrect item can lead to wasted seconds that ruin a leaderboard run. The control scheme is intuitive but requires practice to master during high-pressure timed orders.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Initial Order Intake">
    When you start a shift, check the active customer ticket. It displays the customer's name, order number, and a checklist of toys. If you have multiple orders, use the **R key** to cycle through them and see which items are nearby.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Item Collection">
    Approach a shelf and use **E or Left Click** to pick up an item. If you pick up the wrong toy, press **Q** to drop it immediately. Dropping it near its original shelf is better than carrying it to the packing station.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Product Verification">
    Many items look similar. Use **Right Click or the Y button** to inspect the item in your hands. This opens a small preview window confirming the item's name, ensuring you don't pack a "Red Squishy" when the customer wanted a "Red Bouncer."
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Packing and Delivery">
    Once your inventory is full, return to the packing station. Use the **Scroll Wheel** to select the correct item from your carried inventory and press **E** to place it in the box. Once the checklist is complete, grab the box and load it onto the truck.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Input | Action | Practical Walkthrough Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| E / Left Click | Interact / Pack | Stand close to the target to ensure the action registers |
| Q | Drop Item | Only drop items if your Carry Capacity is full |
| R | Cycle Orders | Check all orders to group items from the same aisle |
| Scroll Wheel | Cycle Inventory | Switch to the next item while walking to the box |
| Right Click / Y | Inspect | Essential for items with similar color palettes |

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Duplicate Work">
In multiplayer, always communicate which order you are working on. There is no penalty for cycling orders, so make sure two players aren't chasing the same "Matcha Dumpling" for the same box.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Co-op Strategies for Leaderboard Dominance">

While playing solo is a great way to learn the ropes, **Pack an Order!** shines in multiplayer. Coordinated teams can divide the warehouse into zones, ensuring that the delivery truck never sits idle. To hit the top of the leaderboards, your team should adopt specific roles.

**Recommended Team Roles:**
- **The Picker**: Stays in the aisles. Their job is to grab items and move them to a central "staging area" or directly to the Packer. They should focus on **Speed** and **Reach** upgrades.
- **The Packer**: Stays at the packing station. They cycle through the orders using **R** and call out what is needed. They are responsible for the final verification of items.
- **The Loader**: Focuses on the final stage of the workflow. They take completed boxes to the truck and help the Picker during downtime.

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Advanced Efficiency Tips and Farming">

To maximize your Coin earnings per hour, you need to transition from "finishing orders" to "farming orders." This requires a shift in mindset where every movement is calculated.

| Farming Phase | Focus Area | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Early Farming | Layout Mastery | Focus on 100% accuracy over raw speed |
| Mid-Tier Farming | Upgrade Loop | Reinvest every coin into Speed immediately |
| High-Tier Farming | Route Optimization | Group orders by shelf proximity |
| Pro Farming | Leaderboard Runs | Use Abilities to bypass travel time |

**Advanced Walkthrough Tactics:**
- **Batch Processing**: Instead of finishing one order at a time, look at all active tickets. If three orders need "Red Squishies," grab all of them in one trip if your Carry Capacity allows.
- **The Staging Method**: If the packing station is busy, drop items in a neat pile nearby. This allows the Packer to grab them instantly without moving into the aisles.
- **Inventory Cycling**: Use the scroll wheel while walking. Never wait until you are standing at the box to start looking for the right item in your inventory.

<Callout type="tip" title="Strategy: The 180-Degree Turn">
When picking up an item, immediately jump and turn 180 degrees. This small mechanical trick helps you maintain momentum toward the packing station the moment the pickup animation finishes.
</Callout>
